1. App Overview & Vision 
1.1 What is CampX? 
CampX (name subject to change) is a student-exclusive social ecosystem platform designed for 
undergraduate and postgraduate college students aged 18 and above. It is a closed, verified 
environment where only enrolled students can participate — making it fundamentally different from 
open platforms like Instagram or LinkedIn. 
 
CampX is not just a social feed. It is a multi-layered platform that combines social networking, 
academic communities, project collaboration, campus events, and premium identity features — all 
built exclusively around student life. 
 
One-Line Vision 
CampX is a platform for students to explore and level up their knowledge, skills and connections, and 
engage in activities and contests. 
 
1.2 The Problem CampX Solves 
Students today are scattered across multiple platforms — WhatsApp for college groups, Instagram 
for social life, LinkedIn for professional identity, and Discord for communities. None of these 
platforms are: 
 
• Built exclusively for students 
• Restricted to verified college members only 
• Designed around both academic and social student life 
• Capable of connecting students across colleges in a structured way 
 
CampX solves this by bringing everything into one verified, student-only ecosystem. 
 
1.3 What Makes CampX Different 
The core differentiator of CampX is exclusivity and depth: 
 
• Exclusivity:  Only verified students can access the platform. No alumni, no faculty, no outsiders. 
• Multi-layer feeds:  College feed, community feed and explore feed serve different purposes and 
audiences. 
• Community & project system:  Students can join and collaborate in structured communities, 
similar to Discord servers but focused on academic and extracurricular work. 
• Events & contests:  CampX will host and facilitate fests, hackathons and other campus events, 
giving students a reason to stay engaged beyond casual scrolling.

4. Verification Flow 
4.1 Overview 
Verification is the process by which a Basic user becomes a Verified user on CampX. There are two 
distinct verification paths depending on whether the user signed up with a college email or a 
personal email. Verified status unlocks full platform access including College Feed, Community Feed, 
posting, messaging and communities. 
 
4.2 Path A — College Email Verification 
This is the faster, automated verification path for students who have a college-issued email address. 
 
1 User signs up with their college email address. 
2 System automatically detects the college email domain and maps it to the corresponding 
college in the database. 
3 An OTP is sent to the college email address. 
4 User enters the OTP in the app within the validity window. 
5 On successful OTP entry, the user is instantly and automatically marked as Verified. 
6 No manual review is required. No Video KYC is required. 
7 User gains full Verified tier access immediately. 
 
4.3 Path B — Video KYC Verification 
This path is for students who signed up with a personal email address. It involves a manual video-
based identity verification process reviewed by the CampX team. 
 
4.3.1 What the Student Must Do in the Video 
The Video KYC recording must clearly capture all of the following: 
 
• Face clearly visible:  — Student must face the camera directly with clear lighting. No masks, 
filters or obstructions. 
• State name and college:  — Student must verbally state their full name and college name on 
camera. 
• Show college ID card:  — Student must hold up their college-issued ID card to the camera. 
• Hold ID next to face:  — Student must hold the ID card beside their face so both are visible 
simultaneously for identity matching. 
• Answer verbal questions:  — Student will be prompted with a few simple questions during the 
recording (e.g. confirming course and year).

Developer Note — Video KYC UI 
The Video KYC screen must guide the user through each step clearly with on-screen prompts. Each 
requirement (face visible, show ID, hold ID next to face, answer questions) should be shown as a 
checklist the user works through during recording. The recording should be a single continuous video. 
 
4.3.2 Submission & Review Process 
1 User completes Video KYC recording in-app and submits it. 
2 Submission is queued for manual review by the CampX admin team. 
3 CampX team reviews the video within 48 hours of submission. 
4 Team checks: face matches ID card, ID card is valid and belongs to a college, verbal answers 
are consistent, all requirements are met. 
5 Decision is made: Approved or Rejected. 
6 User is notified of the result via Push Notification, In-App Notification, Email and SMS 
simultaneously. 
7 If Approved: user is instantly upgraded to Verified tier and gains full access. 
8 If Rejected: user is informed of the reason and can resubmit. Maximum 3 attempts per week. 
 
4.3.3 Rejection & Resubmission Rules 
Rule Details 
Max Attempts 3 attempts per week. Counter resets every 7 days from first 
submission. 
Rejection Reason User must be informed of the specific reason for rejection (e.g. ID not 
visible, face not matching, incomplete video). 
Resubmission User can resubmit a new video after rejection as long as they have 
attempts remaining. 
Attempt Exhausted If all 3 attempts in a week are rejected, user must wait until the 
weekly counter resets before resubmitting. 
Permanent Rejection If a user is found to be submitting fraudulent or fake ID documents, 
their account is permanently banned with no appeal. 
During Review Period User remains as Basic tier while their KYC is under review. A status 
indicator is shown in-app (e.g. Verification Pending).

6.3 Follow System 
CampX has a follow-based social graph. Users can follow others to see their content in feeds. Profiles 
can also be viewed without following, subject to visibility settings. 
 
• Any Verified, Pro or Plus user can follow another user. 
• Basic users cannot follow others or be followed. 
• Following is one-directional — you can follow someone without them following back. 
• Mutual follows (both following each other) unlock DM access between the two users, subject to

16. College Onboarding 
16.1 Overview 
College Onboarding is the process by which a college is added to the CampX platform and made 
available to students for sign-up. Every student on CampX belongs to a college — so the college must 
exist in the system before any student from that college can register. This entire process is managed 
from the Admin / Founder Dashboard. 
 
Two-Phase Model 
Phase 1 (Launch): Founder manually adds colleges. Phase 2 (Scale): Colleges can submit a self-listing 
request via a public form. All requests are reviewed and approved by the founder before going live. 
 
16.2 College Listing Process 
Phase 1 — Founder Adds Manually 
During the initial launch phase, all colleges on the platform are added directly by the founder from 
the Admin Dashboard. No external application is needed. 
 
1 Founder opens College Management module in Admin Dashboard. 
2 Clicks “Add New College” and fills in all required fields (see Section 16.3). 
3 College is saved with status: Hidden — not yet visible to students. 
4 Founder assigns a Campus Ambassador (Rep) to the college. 
5 Once a Rep is assigned, founder manually sets status to Active / Live. 
6 College is now visible on the platform. Students from that college can sign up and select it during 
registration. 
 
Phase 2 — College Self-Listing Request 
Once CampX scales beyond the initial launch colleges, any college in India can submit a request to be 
listed on the platform through a public-facing form. 
 
1 College representative (typically principal, admin or student welfare officer) fills out the CampX 
College Listing Request form. 
2 Request is received in the Admin Dashboard under College Management > Listing Requests 
queue. 
3 Founder reviews the submission — verifies details, checks legitimacy. 
4 Founder approves or rejects the request. Submitter is notified by email either way.

17. Moderation System 
15.1 Overview 
CampX operates a structured content moderation system to ensure the platform remains safe, 
respectful and authentic for all students. Reports are submitted by users and reviewed by the 
CampX admin team. Actions are applied based on the nature and severity of the violation. All 
moderation decisions are communicated to the affected user. 
 
15.2 Content Violations 
The following types of content are subject to moderation on CampX: 
 
Violation Description 
Hate Speech Content that promotes hatred, discrimination or violence against any 
individual or group based on identity, religion, gender, caste or 
ethnicity. 
Nudity / Sexual Content Any explicit or suggestive content not appropriate for a student 
platform. Strictly prohibited. 
Spam or Fake Content Repetitive, misleading or artificially amplified content. Includes fake 
accounts and bot activity. 
Harassment or Bullying Content targeting an individual to intimidate, threaten or humiliate. 
Includes direct messages used for harassment. 
Impersonation Creating an account or posting content that falsely represents 
another student, faculty member or public figure. 
Misinformation Deliberately false or misleading information presented as fact, 
especially content that could cause real-world harm. 
Copyright Violation Posting content that infringes on intellectual property rights without 
authorisation. 
 
15.3 Reporting Flow 
• Any Verified, Pro or Plus user can report a post, comment or user from the feed, profile or DM 
section. 
• The reporting user selects a violation category from the list above and optionally adds a note. 
• The report is submitted to the CampX admin team queue for review. 
• The reporting user receives a confirmation that the report has been received. 
• The reporting user is notified once the admin team has reviewed and taken action. 
• Reports are anonymous — the reported user is not told who reported them.

15.6 Appeals Process 
Appeals — Permanent Bans Only 
Only permanent bans can be appealed. All other moderation decisions (warnings, post removals,

• The founder personally reviews all permanent ban appeals. 
• The decision on the appeal is final. If the appeal is rejected, no further appeals are accepted. 
• Appeals are reviewed within 7 business days of submission.
